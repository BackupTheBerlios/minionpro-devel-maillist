fontspec.sty has special handlers for euler/lucida. With

--
\documentclass{book}
\usepackage{fontspec,xunicode,xltxtra}
\setromanfont[Mapping=tex-text]{Minion Pro}
\makeatletter
   \zf at math@eulertrue
\makeatother
\usepackage{MnSymbol}
\begin{document}
   Hallo $a+b=c$
\end{document}
--

MnSymbol is used, but latin and Greek letters are set in CM (the math  
glyphs that usually are contained in the OML encoding in normal TeX).  
Will's unicode-math seems to be tightly bound to Cambria right now, I  
couldn't get it to work with Minion. Better if this topic moves to  
the XeTeX mailing list, I suppose.

I'll update promptly. I have a couple of questions on MnSymbol 1.4  
(I'm still using 1.3):

1) As I intend to use the outlines version, I don't have to enable  
the map as MixedMap, right?

2) I see open type fonts (otf), but no indications of how to use  
them. I ask this above all because I was surprised to discover that  
even MnSymbol 1.3 can be used with XeTeX with the following combination:

\documentclass{article}

\usepackage{MnSymbol}
\let\mathdollar\undefined
%\usepackage{lucbmath}    %% <-- I'll tell about this below
\usepackage[cm-default]{fontspec}
\usepackage{xltxtra}

\defaultfontfeatures{Scale=MatchLowercase,Mapping=tex-text}
\setmainfont[Numbers=Lowercase]{Warnock Pro} %% I don't mean these  
three match with
\setsansfont{Lucida Sans}                    %% MnSymbol. Just an  
example. XeTeX
\setmonofont{Lucida Sans Typewriter}         %% takes them from the  
system (not TeX)
                                              %% installed fonts.  
Change them as you like.
\begin{document}

\begin{center}
   Roman, {\itshape italics} and {\scshape small caps\dots}\\
   {\sffamily\bfseries Bold sans serif and \itshape bold italic sans  
serif\dots}
\end{center}

\[
   \mathcal F(s) = \int^\infty_0 f(t) \exp(-st)\,\mathrm{d}t
\]

\end{document}

The XeTeX driver *has* to be xdvipdfmx (default in Linux and  
Windows), not xdv2pdf (default in Mac OS X).

Now ---in the formula above, examining character by character with  
Acrobat, \mathcal F is taken from MnSymbol, (s) = from Computer  
Modern, \int^\infty from MnSymbol, _0 f(t) from CM, \exp from  
Warnock, (-st) from CM, \mathrm{d} from Warnock, and t from CM.

Using instead lucbmath (from TUG distro), everything in the formula  
is taken from LucidaNewMath, except _0, \exp and \mathrm{d}, which  
are taken from Warnock.

So I'm thinking with MnSymbol otf maybe could be a better integration  
with XeTeX (Lucida is *not* an otf though ---I don't know why it  
works better).

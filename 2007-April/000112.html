Greetings, I read this list, and have a comment: 

Of the commercial typefaces mentioned above (Garamond, Galliard,
Warnock), I want to tell that I regard Warnock to be in a class for
itself for mathematical texts (maybe close to or even equally good as
Minion):

1) Warnock does possess much of the elegance of Minion, at the same time
Warnock is more robust than Minion (moving on the scale a bit in the
direction towards Utopia/Fourier.) Warnock Caption seems to me to be
maybe more readable in very small sizes than is Minion Pro caption, and
Warnock may, I believe, put lesser demand on printer technology quality
than does Minion.
2) Warnock seems to be very complete (Greek and often missed ligatures
like fj and ffj are there.)
3) Warnock has a very readable italic (in contrast to Garamond Pro,
which I find completely unsuitable for mathematics.)
4) Warnock in normal text sizes feels rather neutral (like Minion and
Kepler) and do (for my eyes) not call attention to itself (in contrast
to Galliard and Palatino, which I like for many purposes, but not for
educational texts.)

So, while I feel that Minion Pro for mathematics do fill what would else
be a whole in the set of available math typefaces, I would personally be
happy if among the other fonts mentioned above, Warnock would be chosen
if someone is going to use time for parallel development.

Best wishes to all for future development and thanks a lot for all the
time used and good work done.

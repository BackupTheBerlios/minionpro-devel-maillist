Ben M. wrote:
> I don't know why this is happening, but based solely on the fact that
> there are enc-vX.XXX.zip files, my guess is that the encoding changed.
> Is this a reasonable assumption?

Yes, Adobe changed the glyph names in the font.

> I compared the OTFs in character map, and there's a character that
> looks just like \upell at U+2113hex.  (See the attached images.)

What is the PostScript name of the glyph? You can find out, for
instance, with a font editor like fontforge.

> So it looks like the unicode character remains unchanged.  The only
> place I see \ell in the entire package is MinionPro.sty:
>   \DeclareMathSymbol{\ell} {\mathord}{letters}{'140}
>   \DeclareMathSymbol{\upell} {\mathord}{letters}{'340}
> I certainly don't understand what's going wrong.  The .enc files don't
> look to me like they convey the information necessary to fix this
> problem.  Do I also need to replace the metrics or something?

No, you only need to update the encoding files.

I just noticed that the glyphlist file changed in newer versions of lcdf
typetools. Try replacing the /lscript in enc/mn-oml.enc with /lsquare.
